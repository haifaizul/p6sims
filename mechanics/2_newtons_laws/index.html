Newton's Laws & Equations

Newton's Three Laws

  • First Law (Inertia): An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion with constant velocity unless acted upon by a net external force.
  • Second Law (F=ma): The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass. F = ma
  • Third Law (Action-Reaction): For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Key Equations

  • Net Force: F_net = F_applied - F_friction
  • Friction Force: F_friction = μ × m × g
  • Acceleration: a = F_net / m
  • Velocity: v = v₀ + at
  • Position: x = x₀ + v₀t + ½at²

Free Body Diagram (FBD)

A free body diagram shows all forces acting on an object. In this simulation, you can see the applied force (blue), friction force (red), and the resulting net force determines the motion.

Simulation Tips

  • Apply a positive force to move the block right, negative to move it left.
  • Increase friction to see how it opposes motion.
  • Change mass to observe how it affects acceleration for the same force.
